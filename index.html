The problem

People want a plan. What they get is friction.

You know you should be saving and investing. But between rent, bills, and everything else, it’s easy to postpone the setup work and fall back into “I’ll do it next month.”

Too many steps

Opening accounts, picking products, scheduling transfers, tracking everything manually — every extra click is a reason to delay.

Tools that guilt you

Many apps tell you what you “should” be doing, then ping you with alerts when you fall behind. That creates stress, not progress.

Life is not a spreadsheet

Some months are smooth, some aren’t. Static savings rules don’t adjust when cash is tight or when you could comfortably save more.

How Growspent works

Automate the boring parts of money.

Growspent connects to your existing accounts and uses tiny amounts from everyday spending to build savings and investments — with guardrails that protect your cash flow.

1
Connect your accounts

Link your bank and cards securely through a regulated data partner. Growspent reads transaction amounts, not what you buy.

2
Choose your rules

Set round-ups, fixed percentages (for example 3–7% of spending), or monthly caps. Decide how much goes to savings, debt paydown, and long-term investing.

3
Let it run on autopilot

Growspent monitors your cash flow, easing off when your balance is low and gently increasing contributions when there is room to save.

Why Growspent

A plan that respects your real life.

Less “do more” pressure. More automation, guardrails, and clear visibility into the progress you’re making from the spending you already do.

  • Cash-flow-aware automation: contributions pause or slow down when your account is low, so you’re not surprised by transfers.
  • Multiple goals from one stream of spending: split each contribution between emergency savings, debt paydown, and investing.
  • Simple, transparent view: see exactly how much of your monthly spending was converted into long-term progress.
  • No day-trading or hot tips: the focus is on steady, diversified growth rather than chasing the latest market story.
